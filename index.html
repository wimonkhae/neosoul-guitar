Learn to Play Neo Soul Guitar

Neo-soul is a subgenre of contemporary R&B that merges the classic soul sounds of Stax and Motown Records with grooves borrowed from hip-hop, rock, funk, and electronic music. Major stars of the neo-soul movement like Alicia Keys, Lauryn Hill, John Legend, and Frank Ocean evoke the sounds of 1960s crooners like Marvin Gaye, Stevie Wonder, and the Reverend Al Green.

Guitar is the foundational harmonic instrument in many neo-soul, R&B, and lofi hip-hop songs. Neo soul guitar tones are most often clean and articulate, with a focus on clarity – so those beautifully complex chord voicings really sing – rather than power.

The chord progression is the most important thing to get right in neo-soul guitar. It trumps rhythm, melody, and any other musical element. Fundamentally, neo-soul chord progressions are characterised by the use of extended chords – minor 7 and major 7 chords in particular.

The easiest way to add a neo-soul flavour to any song is simply by taking a simple chord progression and adding in minor and major 7s.
